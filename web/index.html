Strumento.

The display I always wanted for
La Marzocco espresso machines

An analog boiler-temp dial, a shot timer that takes over the moment you pull the paddle, and easy-to-reach controls for power, steam, pre-brew and backflush. Runs on a $50 M5Stack Core2.

Install

Your browser can't talk to serial ports. Open this page in Chrome or Edge on a desktop. Web Serial needs HTTPS — open the hosted page, not a local file.

Plug your Core2 in over USB-C, click Connect, pick the USB Single Serial / CP2104 port, then Install Strumento. Takes about 90 seconds.

You'll need

  • An M5Stack Core2 — about $50. The v1.1 works too.
  • The La Marzocco app account you already have, with your machine paired.
  • Chrome or Edge on desktop — Safari and Firefox don't do Web Serial.

First boot

  1. The device lands on the Setup screen.
  2. Tap each row and type your WiFi name & password, then your La Marzocco email & password. Leave the serial blank to auto-discover.
  3. Tap Reconnect. The boiler dial goes live within a few seconds; pull the paddle and it flips to the shot timer.

Credentials stay on the device and are only used to log into your WiFi and La Marzocco's cloud.

Supported machines

  • Linea Mini
  • Linea Mini R
  • Linea Micra
  • GS3 AV
  • GS3 MP

The UI checks the machine's capability flags and hides anything that doesn't apply, so whatever the official app can drive should work here too.
